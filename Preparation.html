How can I be well prepared to be a Computer Science major?

A great way to prepare is by getting started now. There are many free sources available online that can introduce you to the basic concepts in programming, web development, and other areas of CS. Once you have chosen a resource to learn from, you want to make sure you are putting what you've learned into practice in the form of projects.

Here are some great resources:

  • Harvard's CS50
  • MIT Open Courseware
  • Edx.org
  • Khan Academy

In a more general sense, you can also prepare by making sure your foundation in mathematics is strong. Not all jobs in Computer Science require a lot of math but having that foundation will allow you to keep your options open. Artificial Intelligence, for example, uses concepts from statistics and discrete mathematics. If you are interested in game programming, you might want to check out Linear Algebra.
